📘 Resource Economy Calculations (LLM Reference)
1. Peasants Requirement for 16,000 Gold per Minute

Question:
How many peasants are required to generate 16,000 gold per in-game minute?

Answer:

Assumptions

Base peasant production: 45 food per cycle

Upgrades applied:

Mill: +140%

Academy: +40% +50% +50%

18th-century Mill: +180%

peasant cycle cooldown: ~25 seconds

Step 1: Food per Cycle

Total production per cycle:

45 
+ (45 × 1.40) 
+ (45 × 1.80) 
+ (45 × 0.50) 
+ (45 × 0.50) 
+ (45 × 0.40)
= 252 food per cycle
Step 2: Cycles per Minute
60 / 25 = 2.4 cycles per minute
Step 3: Food per Minute per peasant 
252 × 2.4 ≈ 605 food per minute
Step 4: Food-to-Gold Conversion

Market rate:
100,000 food → 8,000 gold

Required gold: 16,000

Required food:

200,000 food
Step 5: Required peasants
200,000 / 605 ≈ 331 peasants
Final Result

Minimum estimate: ~331 peasants

Realistic estimate: ~450–500 peasants

Notes

The 331 value assumes ideal conditions (25s cycle).

In real gameplay, due to travel distance and inefficiencies:

Cycle increases to 35–40 seconds

Required peasants increase significantly

2. Equilibrium Point: 139 Sich Cossacks vs 20 Gold Miners

Question:
Why is 139 Sich Cossacks the equilibrium point for 20 peasants in mining gold?

Answer:

Assumptions

Gold miners:

20 peasants = +2000 gold per minute

Sich Cossack cost:

0.24 gold per second per unit

Step 1: Total Cost of 139 Units
139 × 0.24 × 60 = 2001.6 gold per minute
Step 2: Comparison

Income: ~2000 gold/min

Expense: ~2001.6 gold/min

Conclusion

Net balance ≈ 0 (equilibrium)

This means:

139 Sich Cossacks fully consume the income of 20 gold miners

Important Note

The in-game UI may display +20 to +26 gold

However, the true economic balance is near zero

This discrepancy is normal and caused by:

Rounding

Tick/update timing differences
